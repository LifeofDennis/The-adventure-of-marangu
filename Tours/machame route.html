Machame Route ("Whiskey Route")

One of Kilimanjaro's most scenic and popular routes

Quick Facts

Difficulty: Moderate to Challenging
Duration: 6-7 days
Success Rate: ~85% (for 7-day option)
Scenery: Excellent
Crowds: Moderate
Accommodation: Tented camps

Route Overview

The Machame Route, affectionately known as the "Whiskey Route," is renowned as one of the most scenic paths up Mount Kilimanjaro. This route offers trekkers diverse landscapes, from lush rainforests to alpine deserts, and provides excellent acclimatization opportunities with its "climb high, sleep low" profile.

Detailed Itinerary

Day 1: Machame Gate to Machame Camp

Elevation: 1,640m to 2,990m | Distance: 11km | Hiking Time: 5-7 hours

Your adventure begins at Machame Gate (1,640m) with a steady climb through dense rainforest. The trail can be muddy and slippery, so good footwear is essential. You'll arrive at Machame Camp (2,990m) in the late afternoon.

Day 2: Machame Camp to Shira Camp

Elevation: 2,990m to 3,840m | Distance: 5km | Hiking Time: 4-6 hours

As you leave the rainforest, the vegetation changes to heather and moorland. Enjoy spectacular views of Kibo Peak and the Shira Plateau.

Day 3: Shira Camp to Barranco Camp via Lava Tower

Elevation: 3,840m to 3,960m | Distance: 10km | Hiking Time: 6-8 hours

This challenging day takes you up to 4,630m at Lava Tower for acclimatization before descending to Barranco Camp, featuring the famous Barranco Wall.

Advantages

  • Excellent acclimatization profile
  • Most scenic route on Kilimanjaro
  • Higher success rates than Marangu
  • Less crowded than Marangu Route
  • Gradual ascent pattern

Challenges

  • Steeper climbs than Marangu
  • Tented accommodation (no huts)
  • Longer summit day than other routes
  • Can be cold at higher camps
